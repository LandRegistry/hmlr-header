Header and footer

This template is for pages that have no navigation or service name.

Header

The header uses the GOV.UK generic header component. It contains:

  • our picture logo, placed in accordance to the generic header guidance
  • our organisation's name, following the guidance for which font to use when you can't use GOV.UK's Transport font. This is font-family: 'Helvetica Neue', Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif;
  • a black background that matches the generic header. Other brand colours can be used across government however black was most suitable for our organisation's logo

The design ensures our header aligns as closely as possible to the generic header and therefore what other departments will also implement for their services not hosted on GOV.UK.

Footer

There is not yet a generic footer component however we have adapted the GOV.UK footer to comply with the rules for using GOV.UK's Frontend without GOV.UK branding.

The footer contains:

  • navigational links if required
  • the 'Open Government Licence' logo and statement, if applicable for your service
  • the 'Crown copyright' which should always be present/li>
  • the crest which should always be present
  • a black top border that matches the chosen black brand colour
  • a tint of the black for the background that ties into our chosen brand colour of black

Navigational links in the footer do not have to be the same across all services, however where we can make order and link descriptions consistent we should aim to do so. The suggested order and descriptions are based on GOV.UK guidance and a perceived (not proven) need from some of our services to place contact at the beginning:

  • Contact
  • Privacy
  • Accessibility
  • Cookies
  • Terms and conditions
  • Freedom of information

Using GOV.UK components for the header and footer with minimal to no customisations ensures consistency across government and reduces future maintenance.
